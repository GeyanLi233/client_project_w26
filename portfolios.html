Portfolio Toolkits

Successful Portfolios Help You Succeed

A portfolio is a collection of your past work, illustrating your process of delievering high-quality projects and showcasing your capabilities, professional skills and experience.

Portfolio Difference For All Pathways

Generally, portfolios are required for User Experience pathway students when applying for a job. For Data pathway students, portfolios are hardly required. Portfolios are typically not required for Library/Archives and Health Informatics students. For more details, you may refer to Portfolios For All Pathways

Create Your Own Portfolio - Portfolio Components Explained

  • Font Page: keep your font page clean and attractive. Provide information on three completed pojects on this page to intrigue your potential employers.
  • About Me: this is where your show a bit of your personality and gets your employers to know you.
  • Projects: You demonstrate your work in depth in this part. For each projects you put on your portfolio, first make a summary of your role, client, duration and problem or challenge of that project.
  • Navigations and Links: employers have limited time navigating your portfolio, so try to make the navigation easier and keep the links accessible.
