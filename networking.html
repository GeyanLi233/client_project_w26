Networking Guidlines

Connect. Engage. Excel

Networking at UMich is more than just "meeting people"—it’s about leveraging the largest living alumni network in the world. Whether you are a freshman or a graduating senior, use these guidelines to build your professional community.

What is networking and why is it important?

Networking is the proactive process of building professional relationships to share information, resources, and support. At the University of Michigan, it is a critical skill that bridges the gap between your academic studies and your future career.

Why is networking important?

  • Access to the Hidden Job Market: Many positions are filled through referrals before they are ever posted publicly. Networking gives you a "first look" at these opportunities.
  • Industry Insights: Speaking with professionals provides firsthand knowledge about company culture, daily responsibilities, and necessary skills that textbooks cannot provide.
  • The Michigan Advantage: Engaging with the U-M alumni network connects you to a global community of leaders who are uniquely motivated to help fellow Wolverines succeed.

How do I build network and connections?

Building a powerful network is about consistency and curiosity. Here are some effective techniques to help you get started:

  • Request Informational Interviews: Ask alumni for 15 minutes to learn about their career path.
  • Leverage U-M Platforms: Use UCAN and Handshake to find mentors who are already eager to help.
  • Perfect Your Pitch: Have a 30-second "elevator pitch" ready for career fairs and spontaneous meetings.
  • Provide Value: Always send a personalized thank-you note and share relevant articles or updates to stay on their radar.
